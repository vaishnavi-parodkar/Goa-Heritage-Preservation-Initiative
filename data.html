Explore Goa's Timeless Heritage

These iconic sites tell the story of Goa’s rich cultural and architectural history.

Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is one of the most iconic churches in Goa and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built in 1605, it houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a revered Catholic missionary. The church is renowned for its exquisite Baroque architecture, intricately carved altars, and historical significance. Its richly decorated interior and centuries-old legacy make it a must-visit monument, attracting thousands of tourists and pilgrims from around the world each year.

Se Cathedral

The Se Cathedral, located in Old Goa, is one of the largest churches in Asia and a prominent example of Portuguese-Manueline architecture. It was built to commemorate the victory of the Portuguese over the Muslim rulers of the region in 1510, under the leadership of Afonso de Albuquerque. Dedicated to Saint Catherine of Alexandria, the cathedral took over 80 years to complete and was consecrated in 1640. This grand structure is known for its impressive Gothic-style architecture, with Tuscan exteriors and Corinthian interiors. The cathedral once had two towers, but one collapsed in 1776 and was never rebuilt. The highlight of the interior is the “Golden Bell,” which is famed for its rich tone and is one of the largest in Goa. Se Cathedral stands as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and serves as a symbol of Goa's colonial legacy and religious harmony. It continues to attract tourists and devotees from around the world for its spiritual significance and architectural beauty.

Shri Mangeshi Temple

The Shri Mangeshi Temple, situated in the village of Mangeshi in Ponda, is one of the most revered and iconic Hindu temples in Goa. Dedicated to Lord Mangesh, an incarnation of Lord Shiva, the temple is known for its elegant Goan temple architecture, including a seven-story deepstambha (lamp tower) and beautiful domes. Originally located in Kushasthali (Cortalim), the deity was shifted to its current location during the Portuguese era to protect it from destruction. The temple is a major center of pilgrimage and spiritual heritage in Goa.
